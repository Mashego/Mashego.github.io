    Our Story

    Who we are

    SAOPHA(South African Organisation for the Prevention of Transmission of HIV/AIDS, TB & STIs) is a registered South African NPO that focuses on the development of South African youth and training of community healthcare workers, child and youth care workers, as well as social and auxilary workers. We have more than 20 years of experience in implementing care support programmes for chronic disease, and other health programmes.

    Empowerment through Connection

    Our journey at SAOPHA is one born from the need to develop our community. In our endeavours to enrich the lives of South African youth, our mission is simple - be at the service of the children in our communities to build sustainable futures for each child. For us to achieve this mission, we work in partnership with community healthcare workers to create value-based wellness programmes that build individual capacity and promote holistic development. Our youth health programmes provide safe spaces for youth to learn about the importance of self-development to improve their own health outcomes and the health and wellbeing of their families and communities.

    Our Purpose

    Our passion to empower is deeply rooted in the belief that our society is only as good as the children we raise. We believe that in order for our communities to progress, there needs to be an investment in the youth who mature to contribute towards it. That is why we believe SAOPHA is more than just an NPO. Our purpose to empower every child to achieve their fullest potential is not just a goal - it is who we are.

    Youth Development

    SAOPHA works in partnership with all community stakeholders including, but not limited to, the Department of Health, Department of Arts, Culture, Sports and Recreation in Moretele Deistrict. the Department of Education, Gauteng Muninicipality, Church leaders, Youth-led Organisations, Parents and other community stakeholders to implement youth development programmes. The primary beneficiaries of the programmes are young people aged 10 - 24. Our Programmes focus on the Following key pillars to promote holistic development:

    • Mental and Physical Development.

    Adolescence is a vital period for developing and maintaining social and emotional habits important for mental well-being. As a time of change, the youth of today often experience this vulnerable stage in the wake of growing social media pressures, rising unemployement, substance abuse, cyber bullying, and many others. In turn, many mental illnesses including depression, anxiety, eating disorders, substance use disorders and psychosis, start to appear before the age of 24 years. Our youth development programmes engage young people using proven counselling methods, psycological sessions, as well as self-awareness and self-esteem exercises to help them maintain a positive sense of self, relieve emotional strain, and deal with mental trauma proactively.

    In conjuction with mental health development, our programmes also focus on physical health. Regular physical activity promotes growth, the development of soft skills, and reduces the risk of disease. Our Sessions are tailored with a variety of activities such as fun fitness games, sports, dance and cultural enrichment activities(music, fine and performing arts) to provide opportunities to practice skills and build confidence that promote ongoing engagement in physical activity.

    • HIV, Sexual and Reproductive Health

    Sexual education is often an area neglected by parents and other family members. As a result, young people learn about bodily development, sexuality, and relationships from a variety of misinformed and harmful sources. Our youth programmes include comprehensive sexual education sessions to improve sexual and reproductive health outcomes. The following topics are addressed:

    • Abstinence
    • Gender-Based Violence(GBV)
    • Proper condom use and sexual hygiene
    • Relationships
    • HIV/AIDS and other STIs, and
    • LGBTQ


    • Leadership

    The average adoloscent today is exposed to more information than previous generations. With the advent of social media, now anyone with an internet connection can have a voice to spread opinions with the click of a button. And while this may present opportunities, it also provides a platform to spread misinformation, cyber abuse, and hate. Our youth programmes focus on developing key professionals lives, as well as social media. Our Programmes cover topics on developing leadership skills essential to being productive and responsible citizens in today's digital economy. The following areas are addressed:

    • Communication skills
    • Conflict resolution
    • How to nurture emotional intelligence
    • Facing adversity, risk and failure
    • Developing problem-solving and decision-making skills
    • Identifying your strengths and weaknesses
    • Setting goals
    • How to use motivation to succeed
    • Empathy and acceptance of others


    • Entrepreneurship

    Stimulating entrepreneuship amongst our youth is paramount to eliminating poverty. For us to nurture environments that are inclusive and create jobs, our communities need to provide opportunities that enable young people to be avtive participants in their economic futures. Through our Youth Entrepreneurship Development Prgrammes, youth entrepreneurial skills related to these key areas are developed:

    • Entrepreneurship skills: Include inner disciple, ability to take risk, being innovative, change-orientated and persistence.
    • Technical skills: Technical skills help in producing a successful business product or service. The technical skills that are essential for a successful entrepreneur include operations, budgeting, and marketing.
    • Management skills: Management skills are the skills that help executing important decisions. The management skills that are addressed are planning, decision making, and motivation.
    • Personal skills: These include self-awareness, accountability, creativity, and emotional skills.

    Training

    SAOPHA provides accredited community training sessions for community healthcare workers, child and youth care workers, as well social and auxiliary workers. As a SETA accredited training provider, our courses comply with NQF standards and have been evaluated to ensure healthcare workers receive the highest quality education and skills training. Our comprehensive training curriculum consists of three core components which include theiretical workshops, practical implementation, and onsite support and ongoing assessment. Our training workshops can be delivered in-person or through our e-learning system that also hosts various training resources and case-based activities on HIV/AIDs and other communicable diseases, GBV, chronic illness, mental health, and sexual and reproductive education.

    • Increased competence and knowledge on how to facilitate the holistic development of young persons.

    • Have the ability to evaluate health care delivery systems, including eligibility and community resources in order to provide appropriate linkage services to clients.
